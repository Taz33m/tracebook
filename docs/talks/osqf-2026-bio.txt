Taz33m is the creator and maintainer of Tracebook, an open-source toolkit for
matching-engine conformance testing and reproducible failure analysis. The
project focuses on deterministic order-lifecycle traces, semantic differential
testing, delta debugging, profile-scoped qualification, and CI-ready regression
artifacts across Python, Rust, and Go engines. Through Tracebook, Taz33m has
worked with matching-engine maintainers to verify queue-priority contracts,
document profile boundaries, and translate large correctness failures into
small, inspectable reproductions. Recent work connected Flash's broad matching-
engine benchmark to a four-event regression for a historical orderbook-rs FIFO
defect, qualified independently authored engines through a public process
protocol, and tested research-guided trace generation against held-out defects
before deciding not to ship it. Taz33m's broader interest is building rigorous,
accessible infrastructure at the intersection of market microstructure,
distributed systems, and open-source quantitative finance. The goal is to make
correctness evidence easier for engine authors to inspect, challenge,
reproduce, and retain without turning scoped results into certification claims.
